Educational: How to Calculate Age Manually

Curious about the math? Calculating age manually involves a subtraction of the current date from the birth date. However, because dates aren't decimal, you often need to "borrow" from months or years. Here is a step-by-step example:

Example Scenario:
Birth Date: January 1, 2000
Current Date: May 20, 2026

  1. Step 1: Calculate Days
    Subtract birth day (1) from current day (20).
    Result: 19 Days. (If current day were smaller, you would borrow 30 or 31 days from the current month).
  2. Step 2: Calculate Months
    Subtract birth month (1) from current month (5).
    Result: 4 Months. (If current month were smaller, you would borrow 12 months from the current year).
  3. Step 3: Calculate Years
    Subtract birth year (2000) from current year (2026).
    Result: 26 Years.

Final Result: 26 Years, 4 Months, and 19 Days.
